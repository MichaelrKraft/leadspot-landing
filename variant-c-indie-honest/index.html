Why your CRM is empty (and why it's not your fault)

You've tried HubSpot Free. You've tried Notion. You've tried a Google Sheet with 12 columns and color coding. They all start clean. They all rot within a month.

It's not because you're undisciplined. It's because the act of logging is competing for the same brain you're using to actually sell. Every CRM update is a 30-second context switch from the work that makes the money. Multiplied by 40 touchpoints a week, that's two hours of context switching to maintain a record nobody is rewarding you for.

The honest answer is that CRMs were designed for sales managers who wanted to audit reps. They were not designed for the founder who is also doing the selling.

What Ghostlog actually does.

It runs in the background and watches the apps you're already using — Gmail, LinkedIn, Slack, Calendly, Zoom. When it sees a person who's in your CRM, it logs the interaction. That's it.

You never click anything. You never type anyone's name into the CRM. You just work, and the record fills itself in.

FAQ

I'm worried about a daemon watching my screen all day. Talk me out of it.

I'm worried about it too — that's why the architecture is what it is. Screen capture is processed entirely on your Mac. Screenshots are encrypted with a key in your Keychain and never leave the machine. The cloud only receives short summaries like "exchanged email with jane@acme.com at 11:42." Banking apps, password managers, and health portals are muted at the OS level and can't be enabled. You can mute any app at any time. There's a local audit log so you can see exactly what was sent.

Why $39 and not $20 or $99?

$20 is unsustainable — I'd burn out and shut it down within 18 months. $99 is more than the value most solo founders feel they get from a CRM. $39 is the price I think is fair for a year-one bet, and it's locked in for life if you're in the first 100. When v2 ships and ARR justifies it, list price will probably be $79.

Does it work without LeadSpot?

Right now, no. LeadSpot is included in the price. If enough people ask for HubSpot or Attio integration, I'll build it — but I'm not going to ship a half-baked integration just to claim "it works with everything."

Is this just Gong for cheaper?

No. Gong watches calls. Ghostlog watches the entire desktop — emails, DMs, calendar, calls, anything. Gong assumes you have a sales team. Ghostlog assumes you don't.

What other AI stuff does this thing actually do?

Glad you asked — this is honestly the part I underplay because auto-logging is the loud feature. The rest:

• Scores leads from real signals. Not "this person works at a 100-person company so they're a 7." More like — you've exchanged 14 messages, they opened your last 3 emails, similar profiles close in 21 days. That's a real score.

• Drafts your follow-up emails. Reads the actual past conversation and writes the next message in your voice. You edit, you don't start from scratch.

• Catches deals about to stall. Pattern-matches against your past wins and losses. "Marcus's response time doubled — 70% of deals like this stall here."

• Plain-English questions. "Which leads went quiet last week?" gets you the actual records, ranked.

• Auto-enriches contacts. Pulls company, role, LinkedIn, recent press the moment a contact appears. You fill out zero forms.

• 3-line summary per contact. Reads everything, gives you the relationship in one breath.

Why pay for this when HubSpot Free exists?

Real answer: HubSpot Free is a fine database. Ghostlog is a CRM that fills itself in and tells you what to do. Open your HubSpot Free right now. Is it accurate? Is it current? If yes — keep using it, no joke. If it's 70% empty and stale (which is the case for almost every solo founder I've shown), that's the gap Ghostlog closes. Plus HubSpot Free isn't AI-first — it has AI tacked on. Ghostlog is built around AI from the foundation up.
